The Problem
"In Bangalore and Mumbai,
5 kilometres is a 2-hour journey.

India's Tier-1 cities don't have a food shortage.
They have a latency problem."
— Roshan, Founder · Ex-Fractal Analytics · ISB PGP
2H

The window is 2 hours. A surplus meal from a corporate event crosses from nutritious to hazardous not from negligence — but because cities were never designed for last-mile food logistics.

40%

40% of India's food is lost between production and consumption. Most restaurant surplus never reaches those who need it — not from lack of intent, but from documentation burden.

0

Zero platforms today automate the Section 80G compliance chain for food donations. Kitchen managers track quantities on paper, if at all.

₹0

Most ESG disclosures are unverifiable. Corporates and restaurant chains want sustainability stories — but have no auditable data layer to back them up.

The Solution

COMPLIANCE
AS A SERVICE

We replaced a long, fragile supply chain with a short, resilient one. RelayOS starts with software and earns its way into hardware — only where the data justifies it.

01 / SAAS CORE
Plug Into Your POS

RelayOS connects to a restaurant's existing POS system. Every meal flagged as surplus is automatically logged, timestamped, and queued for compliance documentation — zero manual entry for kitchen staff.

⚙️
02 / COMPLIANCE ENGINE
Automate 80G & ESG

The platform auto-generates Section 80G tax exemption certificates, monthly ESG impact reports, and surplus forecasts. Restaurants get a real, auditable paper trail that cuts their tax liability and feeds their sustainability disclosures.

📋
03 / HARDWARE LAYER
Deploy Where Data Proves It

Once data density justifies it, RelayOS deploys IoT-enabled cold storage micro-hubs at proven high-surplus intersections. Hardware is not the entry point — it's the reward for data density, funded by SaaS revenue already collected.

🧊
04 / INSURANCE WEDGE
Legally Defensible Safety

IoT sensors serve as underwriting inputs to a certified safe handoff insurance product. The safety layer is legally defensible, commercially monetisable, and very hard for a competitor to replicate quickly.

🛡️
05 / DEMAND ANCHOR
Corporate CSR Integration

Corporate campuses with CSR budgets receive documented proof that their food consumption is linked to surplus redistribution. The demand-side anchor pre-sells the supply-side network before hardware is deployed.

🏢
06 / LAST MILE
500m, Not 5km

Verified, safe food moves the final 500 metres via gig workers and e-bikes — not trucks racing across gridlocked cities. Donors get frictionless drop-off. NGOs get predictable, verified supply.

WHY NOW?

  • India's new ESG reporting mandates for listed companies (BRSR) have created an urgent compliance market that didn't exist 2 years ago — and no one is serving it for food chains.
  • Section 80G reforms have made food donation tax benefits significantly more accessible, but documentation requirements have simultaneously increased — creating the exact compliance gap RelayOS fills.
  • IoT sensor costs have dropped 70% in 5 years, making cold hub hardware economics viable at restaurant-cluster scale for the first time.
  • India's cloud kitchen market is growing at 12% CAGR. These are digital-native businesses with no legacy POS friction — they are the perfect beachhead for SaaS adoption.
  • Zomato and Swiggy have normalized data-driven restaurant operations. Restaurant chains are finally ready to be sold on data-as-an-asset — not just order management.
